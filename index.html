Medial Elbow Pain Program

Stop or reduce any drill that creates pain, numbness, sharp tingling, or symptoms that linger. This page summarizes the video and is not medical advice.

Program Rules

Keep aggravation low

Activity should stay around 0-2/10 pain during the activity and for the next 24 hours. If symptoms exceed that, reduce intensity, volume, or change the movement.

Massage is optional

Soft tissue work may help, but stop it if it irritates symptoms. The video warns that irritated nerves often dislike direct massage.

Give stretches time

Do the stretches even if they do not create immediate relief. The video says benefits may take a couple of weeks.

Load conservatively

Start with two rounds of each loading superset per session. Add a third round only after symptoms tolerate the work.

Stretches

Stretch 1

Flexor Plus Nerve Mobilization

09:39-10:20
  1. Start with the elbow gently bent.
  2. Pull the fingers back until you feel a gentle forearm stretch.
  3. Keep that stretch level while straightening the elbow.
  4. Bend the elbow fully again and repeat smoothly.

Dosage: 2-3 sets of 3-5 reps. One rep means one time with the elbow straight and one time with the elbow bent. Hold each position for 3-5 seconds. Do this multiple times per day as tolerated, keeping discomfort or pain at or below 2/10.

Stretch 2

Pec 90/90 Stretch

10:42-11:56
  1. Place the hand and forearm on a wall with shoulder and elbow at 90 degrees.
  2. Stagger the feet.
  3. Lean forward and rotate away until the pec or front shoulder stretches.
  4. Drive gently with the back foot instead of hanging on the shoulder.

Dosage: 1 set of 2-3 reps per arm at 90 degrees. Hold each rep for roughly 20-30 seconds. Do this multiple times per day as tolerated, keeping discomfort or pain at or below 2/10. If tingling starts earlier, stop there and let the arm settle.

Stretch 3

Pec 120 Stretch

11:56-13:10
  1. Slide the hand higher so the shoulder is around 120 degrees.
  2. The forearm may come off the wall.
  3. Lean forward and rotate away.
  4. Look for a mild stretch in the chest or front shoulder.

Dosage: 1 set of 2-3 reps per arm at 120 degrees. Hold each rep for roughly 20-30 seconds. Do this multiple times per day as tolerated, keeping discomfort or pain at or below 2/10. Avoid pain, numbness, or sharp tingling.

Nerve Glides

Median nerve

Hands Low: Floss Plus Tension

13:55-15:20
  1. Start with elbows flexed and fingers curled into loose fists.
  2. Straighten the elbows, straighten the fingers, and point the palms back behind you.
  3. Look to one side, then the other.
  4. Let one arm tension while the other flosses, then switch.

Dosage: 1-2 sets of 3-4 reps. Start 2-3 times per week, then build toward 4-5 times per week as tolerated. Keep discomfort or pain at or below 2/10. Reduce intensity by relaxing the fingers, bending the elbows slightly, or using less head motion.

Median nerve

Hands High: Floss Plus Tension

15:21-16:10
  1. Start with elbows bent and hands relaxed by your sides.
  2. Straighten the elbows.
  3. Extend the fingers upward as high as comfortable.
  4. Rotate the head side to side.

Dosage: 1-2 sets of 3-4 reps. Start 2-3 times per week, then build toward 4-5 times per week as tolerated. Keep discomfort or pain at or below 2/10. Use less finger height or elbow range if symptoms climb.

Ulnar nerve

Ulnar Nerve Glide

16:28-18:06
  1. Level 1: hands together, lower them until you feel a light stretch, then move in and out.
  2. Level 2: hold the lowered-hand position and look left and right.
  3. Level 3: slide the hands toward one side, then the other.
  4. Level 4: combine hand sliding with head movement, looking away from the glide direction.

Dosage: 1-2 sets of 3-4 reps. Start 2-3 times per week, then build toward 4-5 times per week as tolerated. Keep discomfort or pain at or below 2/10. Use the lowest level that creates a gentle stretch around the elbow, forearm, palm, ring finger, or pinky.

Loading Supersets

Use these as controlled tissue loading, not a max-strength workout. Keep the 24-hour response calm.

Superset 1

Forearm Pronation + Wrist Flexion

19:10-21:37
  1. For pronation, use a hammer, broomstick, PVC pipe, cable, or similar offset load.
  2. Open the hand and let the weight lower to a comfortable position.
  3. Rotate through available pronation range without losing control.
  4. For wrist flexion, support the forearm, move from extension into flexion, and keep the motion even.

Dosage: Perform this superset 2-3 rounds. Do 8-10 reps of forearm pronation, then 8-10 reps of wrist flexion. Rest briefly or do a stretch/nerve mobilization between rounds. Start with 2 rounds per session, 2-3 times per week, then add a third round as tolerated. Keep about 2 reps in reserve and discomfort or pain at or below 2/10.

Superset 2

Supinating Biceps Curl + Finger Curl

21:37-22:54
  1. For curls, start thumbs-forward and supinate as you curl up.
  2. Use both arms together or alternate.
  3. For finger curls, let dumbbells roll down through the fingers until near the end of pinky range.
  4. Curl back into a fist without turning it into wrist flexion.

Dosage: Perform this superset 2-3 rounds. Do 8-10 reps of biceps curls, then 8-10 reps of finger curls. Rest briefly or do a stretch/nerve mobilization between rounds. Start with 2 rounds per session, 2-3 times per week, then add a third round as tolerated. Keep about 2 reps in reserve and discomfort or pain at or below 2/10.

Practical Schedule

Use the per-exercise dosage above. The stretches can be done multiple times per day as tolerated. Start nerve mobilizations two to three times per week, then build toward four to five times per week. Run the loading supersets two to three times per week if symptoms stay within the 2/10 rule.

Keep the first two weeks easy. Use the next-day symptom response to decide whether to progress.
