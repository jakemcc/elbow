## Program Rules

1. Reduce aggravating factors first.
   - Keep painful activity at or below about 2/10 pain during the activity and for the next 24 hours.
   - If pain exceeds that, reduce intensity, volume, or change the movement style.
   - Do not stop all activity by default; adjust the load so the elbow calms down.

2. Optional: soft tissue work.
   - Massage or instrument-assisted soft tissue work is optional.
   - Discontinue it if it irritates symptoms, worsens symptoms, or does nothing useful.
   - The video specifically warns that massaging an irritated nerve can make things worse.

3. Do the stretches even if you do not feel immediate relief.
   - The video says benefits may take a couple of weeks.
   - Use only a mild stretch. Pain should stay around 0-2/10.

4. Keep nerve work gentle.
   - The video recommends doing nerve exercises for at least a couple of weeks, even if you are unsure whether nerves are involved.
   - Nerve drills should create a light stretch in one arm at a time, not pain.
   - A brief symptom increase for a few minutes may happen, but symptoms should not linger.

5. Add loading conservatively.
   - Start with two rounds of each superset per session.
   - Once tolerated, add a third round.
   - Complete all rounds of superset 1 before moving to superset 2.

## Practical Session Layout

The video does not give exact weekly frequency or rep counts for every drill. A conservative way to run it:

- Daily or near-daily: gentle stretches and nerve mobilizations.
- Two to three times per week: loading supersets, if symptoms stay at or below the 2/10 rule.
- Keep the first two weeks easy and use symptom response over the next 24 hours to decide whether to progress.

## Exercises

### 1. Flexor Plus Nerve Mobilization Stretch

Timestamp: 09:39-10:20  
Clip: `clips/01-flexor-nerve-mobilization.mp4`

How to do it:

1. Start with the elbow gently bent in a neutral position.
2. Pull the fingers back until you feel a gentle forearm stretch.
3. Maintain that same stretch level while straightening the elbow.
4. Bend the elbow fully again.
5. Repeat smoothly.

Adjust intensity by pulling the fingers less, straightening the elbow less, or bending the elbow less.

### 2. Pec 90/90 Stretch

Timestamp: 10:42-11:56  
Clip: `clips/02-pec-90-90-stretch.mp4`

How to do it:

1. Place the hand and forearm on a wall or object with the shoulder at 90 degrees and elbow at 90 degrees.
2. Stagger the feet.
3. Lean forward and rotate away until you feel a stretch in the pec or front of the shoulder.
4. Drive gently with the back foot rather than hanging on the shoulder.
5. Do not squeeze the mid-back to force more range.

Hold about 20-30 seconds if tolerated. If tingling starts earlier, stop there, lower the arm, let symptoms settle, and switch sides.

### 3. Pec 120 Stretch

Timestamp: 11:56-13:10  
Clip: `clips/03-pec-120-stretch.mp4`

How to do it:

1. Slide the hand higher so the shoulder is around 120 degrees.
2. The forearm may come off the wall/object.
3. Stagger the feet, lean forward, and rotate away.
4. Look for a mild stretch in the chest or front of the shoulder.
5. Avoid pain, numbness, or sharp tingling.

Hold about 20-30 seconds if tolerated. If tingling appears around 15 seconds or less, use that shorter time.

### 4. Median Nerve, Hands Low: Floss Plus Tension

Timestamp: 13:55-15:20  
Clip: `clips/04-median-nerve-hands-low.mp4`

How to do it:

1. Start with elbows flexed and fingers curled into loose fists.
2. Straighten both elbows.
3. Straighten the fingers and point the palms/fingers back behind you, as if trying to put the palms on the floor.
4. Look to one side. One arm tensions while the other flosses.
5. Look to the other side and let the tension/floss sides switch.
6. Repeat side to side.

To reduce intensity, relax the fingers, bend the elbows slightly, use less head motion, or let the shoulders rise a bit. Keep shoulders down if tolerated.

### 5. Median Nerve, Hands High: Floss Plus Tension

Timestamp: 15:21-16:10  
Clip: `clips/05-median-nerve-hands-high.mp4`

How to do it:

1. Start with elbows bent and hands relaxed by your sides.
2. Straighten both elbows.
3. Extend the fingers upward as high as comfortable.
4. Rotate the head to one side, then the other.
5. Repeat smoothly, alternating which side is tensioning.

To reduce intensity, point the fingers less, keep the elbows slightly bent, or reduce head rotation.

### 6. Ulnar Nerve Glide

Timestamp: 16:28-18:06  
Clip: `clips/06-ulnar-nerve-glide.mp4`

Goal: find a level that gives a light stretch around the elbow, front of forearm, palm, ring finger, or pinky.

Level 1:

1. Place the hands together in front of you.
2. Look straight ahead.
3. Lower the hands until you feel a light stretch.
4. Move in and out of that position.

Level 2:

1. Hold the lowered-hand position.
2. Look left and right to alternate tension and flossing.

Level 3:

1. Go into the lowered-hand position.
2. Slide the hands toward one side, then the other.

Level 4:

1. Combine hand sliding with head movement.
2. Look away from the hand direction you are gliding toward.

Use the lowest level that produces a gentle stretch. If you feel numbness, sharp tingling, or pain, reduce range or stop until symptoms settle.

## Loading Supersets

These are included because the video frames controlled loading as part of recovery. Use light load and avoid form compensation.

### Superset 1: Forearm Pronation + Wrist Flexion

Timestamp: 19:10-21:37  
Clip: `clips/07-pronation-and-wrist-flexion.mp4`

Forearm pronation:

1. Use a hammer, broomstick, PVC pipe, cable, or similar offset load.
2. Open the hand and let the weight lower to a comfortable position.
3. Rotate through available pronation range.
4. Use less load if you get stuck, lose control, or compensate heavily.

Wrist flexion:

1. Support the forearm on a bench or stable surface.
2. Hold the implement with a thumb-under grip.
3. Move from wrist extension into wrist flexion.
4. Keep the motion even and controlled.

### Superset 2: Supinating Biceps Curl + Finger Curl

Timestamp: 21:37-22:54  
Clip: `clips/08-biceps-and-finger-curls.mp4`

Supinating biceps curl:

1. Hold dumbbells with thumbs forward.
2. Curl up while supinating so the palms face inward/upward by the top.
3. Use both arms together or alternate.

Finger curl:

1. Hold dumbbells at your sides.
2. Let them roll down through the fingers until near the end of the pinky range, without losing the weight.
3. Curl the fingers back into a fist.
4. Do not turn it into wrist flexion.

Start with two rounds of each superset per session. Add a third round only after the elbow tolerates the work.
